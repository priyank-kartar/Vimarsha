## P1 — The commuting nonfiction reader ("Arjun")

Reads ambitious nonfiction (economics, history of science, biographies) but his reading
time is the commute + chores. Audiobook editions are missing, expensive, or abridged — and
when a book says "as shown in the chart below," audio leaves him stranded.

- **JTBD:** *When my hands and eyes are busy, help me actually progress through the real
  books I own, without losing the parts that need to be seen.*
- **Hero moment:** the chart appears on the dash-mounted/desk phone exactly when discussed.
- **Willing to pay for:** whole-book narration quality + not having to buy the audiobook twice.

## P2 — The student with a textbook stack ("Mira")

Grad student; reads PDFs-turned-EPUBs and technical texts full of figures and equations.
Re-reads chapters before exams; wants to interrogate passages ("explain this simpler").

- **JTBD:** *Turn dense assigned reading into something I can absorb on walks and review by
  asking questions, with the figures in front of me.*
- **Hero moment:** Discuss explains the paragraph she just heard, grounded in the actual text.
- **Willing to pay for:** Discuss depth + heavy narration volume (a semester's load).

## P3 — The accessibility-first reader ("Dev")

Low vision / dyslexia / reading fatigue. Listening *is* reading; most TTS apps treat
figures, structure, and navigation as afterthoughts and their UIs as forms.

- **JTBD:** *Give me a first-class listening reader for the books I own — structure-aware,
  resumable, navigable by ear, honest about what's on screen.*
- **Hero moment:** eyes-free chapter runs with VoiceOver-coherent controls; figures are
  announced and inspectable.
- **Non-negotiables:** the [accessibility](../03-design/accessibility.md) matrix — Reduce
  Motion/Transparency parity, Dynamic Type XXL, labeled gesture alternatives.

## Anti-persona

The audiobook-catalog consumer who wants professional voice acting for bestsellers and
doesn't own EPUBs — Audible serves them; we don't.

## What the personas agree on (product spine)

Own-files first · narration quality over voice variety · figures are content, not décor ·
conversation must be about *this* passage · no accounts until the cloud genuinely enters.

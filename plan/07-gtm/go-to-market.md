## Wedge

Lead with the demo moment ([positioning](../02-market/positioning.md)): *the chart rises as
the narrator mentions it.* One clip carries the whole pitch; everything funnels people to
watching it.

## Channel hypotheses (rank by persona, test cheap)

1. **BookTok / book-Twitter / Booktube nonfiction corners** (P1 commuters) — the demo clip
   is native content there; creators with "how I read more" content are the multipliers.
2. **Student + studygram/Notion-adjacent communities** (P2) — "turn your textbook stack
   into a podcast that shows the figures"; semester moments (Aug/Jan) are the windows.
3. **Accessibility communities** (P3) — AppleVis, r/Blind, dyslexia orgs: arrive humble,
   with the [a11y matrix](../03-design/accessibility.md) actually verified; this community
   detects lip service instantly and rewards the real thing with the strongest word-of-mouth.
4. **Apple-design circles** (the 🎨 pillar) — design showcases, "Liquid Glass done right"
   posts, App Store editorial pitch (the motion system is editorial-feature bait).

## Launch sequence

1. **Private TestFlight** (M5/M6 era): ~20–50 users across the three personas; instrument
   nothing content-wise — feedback via conversation; exit = retention anecdotes + Q-COST
   reality + crash-free sessions.
2. **Public TestFlight + waitlist page** (the demo clip + email capture): build the launch
   list; seed creators from channel 1/3 with early access.
3. **App Store launch** (P10): coordinated — clip everywhere the same week, Product Hunt,
   App Store editorial pitch submitted ahead, a11y-community posts authored *with* beta
   users from that community.

## What we deliberately don't do at launch

Paid acquisition (COGS-heavy product — organic-fit first) · press exclusives (no leverage
yet) · Android promises · lifetime-deal sites (wrong users, support-heavy).

## Success signals to watch (pre-metrics, beta era)

People send the clip unprompted · beta users finish books (not just chapters) · a11y users
call it *their* reader · "when is Android coming" volume (a good problem, logged not acted).

## The headline ladder

- **H1 (the product):** *Your books, talking.*
- **H2 (the differentiator):** *It shows you the figure the moment it's discussed.*
- **H3 (the relationship):** *And when something doesn't click — ask it.*

The clip *is* the message: narration plays → "as the chart shows…" → the chart rises.
Captions stay out of the way; the moment lands wordlessly.

## Tone

Literate, calm, a little warm — the voice of a good narrator, not a SaaS landing page.
Sentences you could read aloud. No exclamation points, no "supercharge," no "AI-powered"
([positioning](../02-market/positioning.md) banned-words list). Privacy line, exactly:
*"Your books and reading life stay on your phone — narration is processed transiently and
never stored"* ([privacy-security](../04-architecture/privacy-security.md)).

## Objection handling (keep answers this short)

- *"Why not just the audiobook?"* — There isn't one for most books you own. And the
  audiobook can't show you Figure 3.
- *"TTS sounds robotic."* — Listen to ten seconds. (The voice is the answer; never argue
  specs.)
- *"Where does my book go?"* — To a GPU for the minutes it takes to narrate, then it's
  gone. Your library lives on your phone.
- *"Why iOS 26 only?"* — Because the glass is real.
